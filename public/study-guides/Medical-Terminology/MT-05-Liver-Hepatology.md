## 3. Liver Disease Stages

### Hepatitis

- **Definition:** Inflammation of the liver, which may be caused by a viral infection, poisons, or the use of alcohol or drugs
- **Key point:** Inflammation (not scarring)
- **Causes:**
  - Viral infection
  - Toxins
  - Alcohol
  - Drugs
- **ðŸ”‘ Key:** Inflammation = hepatitis

**ðŸ”‘ Exam Trap:** Hepatitis = inflammation, NOT scarring (that's cirrhosis)

---

### Cirrhosis

- **Definition:** Gradual loss of liver function due to cell damage and internal scarring
- **Key point:** Scarring and architectural distortion (not just inflammation)
- **Features:**
  - Chronic scarring
  - Fibrosis
  - Nodular changes
  - Architectural distortion
- **Progression:** Can result from chronic hepatitis
- **ðŸ”‘ Key:** Scarring/fibrosis = cirrhosis

**ðŸ”‘ Exam Trap:** Cirrhosis = scarring (end-stage changes), not inflammation

---

### Liver Failure

- **Definition:** Final state of liver disease, in which liver function becomes so impaired that other areas of the body are affected, most commonly the brain
- **Features:**
  - Functional collapse
  - Multi-organ involvement
  - Hepatic encephalopathy (brain dysfunction)
- **Progression:** End-stage
- **ðŸ”‘ Key:** Functional collapse + multi-organ = liver failure

---

## 4. Hepatitis Types

### Hepatitis A

- **Transmission:** Contact with contaminated food or water (fecal-oral route)
- **Progression:** Does NOT become chronic
- **ðŸ”‘ Key:** Fecal-oral + no chronic = Hepatitis A

---

### Hepatitis B

- **Transmission:** Sexual contact or contact with infected blood or body fluids
- **Progression:** CAN become chronic
- **ðŸ”‘ Key:** Blood/sexual + can be chronic = Hepatitis B

---

### Hepatitis C

- **Transmission:** Blood and sexual contact (similar to Hepatitis B)
- **Progression:** CAN become chronic
- **ðŸ”‘ Key:** Blood/sexual + can be chronic = Hepatitis C

**ðŸ”‘ Exam Trap:** Hepatitis A = food/water, does NOT become chronic. B/C = blood/sexual, CAN become chronic.

---

### Hepatitis D

- **Transmission:** Requires co-infection with Hepatitis B
- **Type:** Defective virus (cannot exist independently)
- **ðŸ”‘ Key:** Requires Hepatitis B = Hepatitis D

**ðŸ”‘ Exam Trap:** Hepatitis D cannot exist without Hepatitis B

## 5. Liver Cancer

- **Also called:** Hepatocellular carcinoma
- **Type:** Malignant
- **ðŸ”‘ Key:** Liver + malignant = hepatoma

## 6. Liver Function Indicators

### Jaundice

- **Definition:** Yellowing of the skin and whites of the eyes because of the presence of excess bilirubin in the blood, usually a sign of a disorder of the liver
- **Cause:** Excess bilirubin (liver dysfunction)
- **Distinction:**
  - **Cyanosis:** Bluish (low oxygen)
  - **Pallor:** Paleness
- **ðŸ”‘ Key:** Yellowing + bilirubin = jaundice

---

### Hepatic Encephalopathy

- **Definition:** Brain dysfunction resulting from liver failure
- **Cause:** Liver failure affecting brain (ammonia accumulation)
- **Progression:** Jaundice â†’ hepatic encephalopathy (liver failure)
- **ðŸ”‘ Key:** Liver failure + brain dysfunction = hepatic encephalopathy

## 8. ABRET Exam Pearls

### Critical Distinctions
1. **Hepatitis vs Cirrhosis:** Inflammation vs scarring
2. **Hepatitis A vs B/C:** Fecal-oral (no chronic) vs blood/sexual (can be chronic)
3. **Hepatitis D:** Requires Hepatitis B (defective virus)
4. **Jaundice vs Cyanosis:** Yellowing (bilirubin) vs bluish (oxygen)

### Common Exam Traps
- Mixing hepatitis (inflammation) with cirrhosis (scarring)
- Confusing Hepatitis A transmission (food/water) with B/C (blood/sexual)
- Forgetting that Hepatitis D requires Hepatitis B
- Confusing jaundice (yellow, bilirubin) with cyanosis (blue, oxygen)
