## 2. Eye Anatomy

### Retina

- **Definition:** A membrane lining the inside of the back of the eye that contains light-sensitive nerve cells (rods and cones) that convert focused light into nerve impulses making vision possible

- **Location:** Back of eye

### Cornea

- **Definition:** A clear dome-shaped front portion of the eye's outer covering
- **Function:** Refracts light (first structure light passes through)
- **Location:** Anterior (front)
- **Characteristics:** Clear, dome-shaped
- **ðŸ”‘ Key:** Clear + dome + anterior + refracts = cornea

---

### Iris

- **Definition:** The colored portion of the eye
- **Function:** Controls pupil size and regulates light entering the eye
- **Location:** Behind cornea
- **ðŸ”‘ Key:** Colored + pupil control + light regulation = iris

---

### Conjunctiva

- **Definition:** A clear membrane covering the white of the eye and the inside of the eyelid that produces a fluid that lubricates the cornea and eyelid
- **Inflammation:** Conjunctivitis (also called "Pinkeye")
- **Location:** Covers eye white and eyelid inner surface
- **ðŸ”‘ Key:** Membrane + lubrication + pinkeye = conjunctiva

## 3. Vision Disorders

### Retinitis Pigmentosa

- **Definition:** Gradual loss of the field of vision due to the degeneration of the light-sensitive nerve cells in the retina
- **Features:**
  - Affects **peripheral vision first**
  - Night blindness (nyctalopia)
  - Eventually causes tunnel vision
  - Central vision preserved until late
- **Fundoscopy:** Bone-spicule pigmentation
- **ðŸ”‘ Key:** Peripheral field loss FIRST + bone-spicule = retinitis pigmentosa

**ðŸ”‘ Exam Trap:** Peripheral first (not central) distinguishes from macular degeneration

---

### Macular Degeneration

- **Definition:** Gradual loss of vision due to deterioration of nerve tissue in the retina in the area that allows for fine details to be observed at the center of vision
- **Features:**
  - Affects **central vision first**
  - Interferes with fine detail discrimination (reading, faces)
  - Peripheral vision usually preserved
- **Fundoscopy:** Drusen deposits
- **ðŸ”‘ Key:** Central vision loss FIRST + drusen = macular degeneration

**ðŸ”‘ Exam Trap:** Central first (not peripheral) distinguishes from retinitis pigmentosa

---

### Retinal Artery Occlusion

- **Definition:** Obstruction of an artery that supplies blood to the retina, resulting in some degree of temporary or permanent blindness
- **Presentation:**
  - Sudden, painless, severe vision loss in one eye
  - Often described as "curtain coming down"
- **Type:** Ophthalmologic emergency
- **ðŸ”‘ Key:** Sudden + painless + severe = retinal artery occlusion

**ðŸ”‘ Exam Trap:** Sudden onset distinguishes from gradual conditions (macular degeneration, retinitis pigmentosa)

## 4. Eyelid Disorders

### Blepharitis

- **Definition:** Inflammation limited to the eyelid margins
- **Features:**
  - Eyelid margin inflammation
  - Often associated with crusting and irritation
- **Location:** Eyelid margins (not the eye itself)
- **ðŸ”‘ Key:** Eyelid margins + inflammation = blepharitis

**ðŸ”‘ Exam Trap:** Eyelid (not conjunctiva) distinguishes from conjunctivitis

---

### Ptosis

- **Definition:** Drooping of the upper eyelid
- **Causes:** Various (nerve, muscle, mechanical)
- **ðŸ”‘ Key:** Drooping eyelid = ptosis

## 5. Eye Movement Disorders

### Nystagmus

- **Definition:** Persistent, rapid, involuntary movement of the eye
- **Types:**
  - Horizontal
  - Vertical
  - Rotary
- **Causes:** Brainstem, cerebellar, vestibular disorders
- **ðŸ”‘ Key:** Involuntary eye movement = nystagmus

## 6. Eye Procedures

### Retinoscopy

- **Definition:** A method of determining focusing errors of the eye in which light is shined through the pupil and the reflected beam is measured
- **Type:** Objective refraction test (does not require patient responses)
- **Purpose:** Determine refractive error
- **ðŸ”‘ Key:** Objective + light reflection = retinoscopy

## 7. Terminology Distinctions

### Ocular vs Optic

#### Ocular
- **Definition:** General term related to the eye
- **Usage:** Broad term for anything eye-related
- **ðŸ”‘ Key:** General eye term

#### Optic
- **Definition:** Refers specifically to the optic nerve or vision pathways
- **Usage:** More specific (optic nerve, optic pathways)
- **ðŸ”‘ Key:** Specific to optic nerve/pathways

**ðŸ”‘ Exam Trap:** Ocular is general; optic is specific to nerve/pathways

## 9. ABRET Exam Pearls

### Critical Distinctions
1. **Retinitis pigmentosa vs Macular degeneration:** Peripheral first vs central first
2. **Blepharitis vs Conjunctivitis:** Eyelid margins vs conjunctival membrane
3. **Ocular vs Optic:** General eye term vs specific to optic nerve
4. **Sudden vs Gradual vision loss:** Sudden = vascular emergency, gradual = degenerative

### Common Exam Traps
- Mixing peripheral (retinitis pigmentosa) with central (macular degeneration) field loss
- Confusing blepharitis (eyelid) with conjunctivitis (membrane)
- Using "optic" for general eye conditions (should use "ocular")
- Missing sudden onset as emergency indicator (retinal artery occlusion)

### Memory Anchors

- Sudden + painless = vascular emergency
- Eyelid = blepharitis, membrane = conjunctivitis
